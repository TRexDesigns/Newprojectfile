DIY Potion Recipe: Don't Worry, Be Happy

PREPARATION: potion bottle with lid (or jar), glitter, feather

OVERVIEW: A worry can be dark and even scary. Follow the instructions and fill the bottle with items that represent those worries. Then find out how the power of magic release those worries.

Go outside and find the following:

  • fill the bottle 1/2 way with something dry and is black or brown, such bark or dirt
  • 1 crooked twig
  • 2 pebbles

    • Sit down, hold the bottle to your chest, look inside the potion bottle and look at the worries inside. Take a deep breath and breathe out the junk. Now find the rest of the items from nature:

  • something yellow to bring those happy feelings and erase the bad
  • something bright

    • Add the Non-Organic Materials:

  • 1 feather
  • 2 pinches of glitter

    • Listen for birds and sit down where you can hear them

  • put the top on your potion and shake it as hard as you can
  • hold the potion bottle close to your chest and say “go away worries, I’m releasing you to the clouds
  • close your eyes and take a deep breath
  • now hold the potion high up to the sky and tell the birds to take the worries
  • Listen closely, do you hear the birds chirping. That means they hear you and are here to help.
  • Good bye worries. Do you feel better?

    Morning Craft


    PREPERATION: The night before I set the craft table with various art supplies for decorating. These items can be crayons, hearts cut outs from a magazine, construction paper, or recycled color book pages, gems, stickers or abstract shapes cut from paper. They will also need a blank canvas to decorate such as a paper pre cut heart, or draw a heart on a piece of cardboard or blank sheet of paper. Lastly they will need glue. We use the purple glue stick that dries clear and silver craft glitter glue. The glitter glue is inside the recycled make up jar (gold top). The bottle is hard to squeeze so she applies the glitter glue with a small paint brush or cotton swab. The main goal is to have the child decorate something shapped like a heart in order to stay within the heart themed activity. Washable Glitter Glue by Craft Smart can be found at Michaels Craft Store or online.

    Educational Play

    We love to play with our Magnetic Human Body Anatomy Playset made by Melissa & Doug. This puzzle comes with over 20 magnetic pieces and helps to visualize the body systems and location of organs. In addition to the major body systems the puzzle comes with male and female layers. This makes the introduction easy to talk about both boy and girl body parts. Give your child some time to independently match the different layers and complete each body system. Then sit down with your child and talk about the vascular or circulatory system and the important role of our hearts. Here are some playful discussion tips:

      Key Facts:
    • Describe the heart as a small, powerful muscle in our chest that works like a pump or an engine. The heart pumps blood around the body delivering oxygen and nutrients it need to function.
    • The blood pumped by your heart travels through arteries (red), and veins (blue). Think of them as roads or playground tunnel slides in our body.
    • Make a fist, hold it to your chest. That’s how big your heart is!

      Your Heart Has Such An Important Job- Here Are Ways To Keep Your Heart Healthy:
    • Keep moving. Physical activity is important to incorporate everyday.
    • Limit screen time. Excessive screen time leads to a sedentary lifestyle and constant snacking. Don't be a couch potatoe.
    • Maintain wellness visits with your doctor.
    • Eat healthy. Whole wheat or grains, apples, low-fat dairy products such as Greek Yogurt and string cheese, poultry, fish and nuts.

    ROAR! I’m Baby Three-Rex, & This Is What The California Department of Education Says About Me

    What I'm Like:

    Watch out! I am charged with physical energy. I do things on my own terms. My mind is a sponge. Reading and socializing are essential in getting me ready for school.  I like to pretend a lot and enjoy scribbling on everything. I am full of questions, many of which are “Why?” I become fairly reliable about using the potty. I may stay dry at night and may not. Playing and trying new things out are how I learn.  Sometimes I like to share. I begin to listen more and begin to understand how to solve problems for myself.

    What I Need:

    I want to know about everything and understand words, and when encouraged, I will use words instead of grabbing, crying, or pushing. Play with me, sing to me, and let’s pretend!
